# Relationship Evidence Evaluation Calculator (REEC)

The **Relationship Evidence Evaluation Calculator (REEC)** is a small experimental tool that encourages more careful and evidence-based thinking when interpreting behaviors in romantic relationships.

Instead of relying on simplistic explanations — such as zodiac compatibility, personality stereotypes, or immediately labeling someone as *"love bombing"*, *"avoidant"*, or *"toxic"* — this tool attempts to frame observations using a **probabilistic behavioral evidence approach**.

The goal is not to judge people, but to help users slow down and think more critically about relationship patterns.

---

# Purpose

Many online discussions about relationships encourage **premature psychological labeling** based on very limited observations.

Common examples include:

- "He didn't reply quickly → avoidant attachment"
- "She shows intense affection → love bombing"
- "Your zodiac signs are incompatible"

These interpretations are often **oversimplified and cognitively biased**.

REEC was created as a small experiment to encourage a different mindset:

**observe behavior, consider uncertainty, and avoid jumping to conclusions.**

---

# What This Tool Does

The calculator allows users to enter **observable relationship behaviors** and then generates **probabilistic pattern indicators** rather than deterministic conclusions.

The tool encourages thinking in terms of:

- evidence rather than assumptions
- probabilities rather than certainty
- behavioral patterns rather than labels

The conceptual inspiration comes from areas such as:

- attachment theory
- relationship science
- social psychology
- probabilistic reasoning
- cognitive bias research

---

# Important Disclaimer

This tool **does NOT diagnose individuals**.

Human behavior is complex, context-dependent, and influenced by many factors that cannot be captured in a simple questionnaire.

Even trained psychologists rarely draw strong conclusions from limited behavioral observations.

The calculator only produces **interpretive estimates based on user-provided inputs**, which may be incomplete, biased, or lacking context.

Results should **never be used as the sole basis for major relationship decisions.**

---

# Limitations

This tool has several important limitations.

## 1. User Input Bias

The analysis depends entirely on what the user chooses to enter.  
If the inputs are biased or incomplete, the results will reflect that bias.

## 2. Limited Context

Real relationships include many contextual factors such as:

- stress
- cultural communication styles
- personality differences
- life circumstances

These cannot be fully captured by a simple behavioral input system.

## 3. Small Observation Windows

Relationship patterns typically require **long-term observation**.  
Short-term behavioral snapshots can easily produce misleading interpretations.

## 4. Human Behavior Is Non-Deterministic

People behave differently depending on emotions, environment, and life events.  
No algorithm can reliably determine someone's motives or psychological traits.

---

# Intended Use

REEC is intended as a **reflection tool**, not a diagnostic instrument.

Possible uses include:

- encouraging more thoughtful interpretation of relationship behaviors
- highlighting uncertainty in interpersonal judgments
- reducing premature labeling of partners
- promoting probabilistic thinking about social interactions

---

# What This Tool Is NOT

REEC is **not**:

- a psychological diagnosis tool
- a clinical assessment
- a relationship decision engine
- a method to determine whether someone is “good” or “bad”

It is simply a small experiment in **behavioral reasoning and analytical humility**.

---

# Why This Project Exists

Many online relationship discussions encourage **fast conclusions with very little evidence**.

This project attempts to promote the opposite:

- slower interpretation
- awareness of uncertainty
- recognition of cognitive bias

Even this tool is imperfect and limited, but the hope is that it encourages people to think **a little more carefully before labeling others.**
