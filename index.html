📖 REEC 2.0 — Manual & Scientific Basis

REEC 2.0 assesses 11 dimensions of relationship dynamics using 48 items rated 0–5 (Never→Always). The scoring uses a centred weighted softmax — neutral answers produce equal baseline probabilities, and reverse-scored items are handled correctly with signed weights.

Scoring Method

Each answer is centred: v = (score − 2.5) / 2.5 → range [−1, +1]. Hypothesis weights may be positive or negative. The weighted dot product is fed into a numerically stable softmax to yield probabilities summing to 100%.

Dimensions

  • D1 Attachment Anxiety — fear of abandonment, hyperactivating strategies (ECR-R, Brennan et al. 1998)
  • D2 Attachment Avoidance — discomfort with closeness, deactivating strategies (ECR-R)
  • D3 Boundary Styles — porous, clear, protective, controlling, respectful (RRBS, Hutchison 2024)
  • D4 Conflict Patterns — harsh startup, contempt, flooding, repair, stonewalling (Gottman 1994)
  • D5 Relationship Evaluation — social comparison, feared future, negative vigilance, past comparison
  • D6 Emotional Intimacy — disclosure, bond, perceived responsiveness, detachment (EIS, Laurenceau et al. 1998)
  • D7 Reciprocity — equity, mutual initiation, under-benefit (Walster et al. 1978)
  • D8 Commitment — dedication, constraint, alternatives, investment (Investment Model, Rusbult et al. 1998)
  • D9 Communication Style — levelling, placating, distracting, computing (Satir 1988)
  • D10 Emotional Regulation — strategies, impulse, clarity, overwhelm (DERS, Gratz & Roemer 2004)
  • D11 Disorganised Attachment — fearful-avoidant conflict, trauma history, rapid shifts, repetition (Main & Hesse 1990)

Attachment Classification

  • Secure — low D1, low D2, low D11
  • Anxious/Preoccupied — high D1, low D2
  • Dismissive-Avoidant — low D1, high D2
  • Fearful-Avoidant — high D1 + D2, or high D11 (disorganised)
